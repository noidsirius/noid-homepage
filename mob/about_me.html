About Me

Early life

My name is "Navid Salehnamadi" (In Persian "نوید صالح‌نمدی") and I was born in December 1st ,1992. I had amazing childhood, I grew up in Tehran (a polluted and populated city) and a village called "Ferizhand". I went to gymnastics and won the gold medal in a competition in our city region. In my primary school, I started to play chess and found it very interesting. Chess taught me how to think in a proper way.

Highs chool

I went to Allameh Helli High School(NODET), a school for talented students. I started to learn about computer science and artificial intelligence by joining a team to compete in 3D Soccer Simulation League (which qualified in International Robocup Competition), then designed and developed a multi-agent solution for Rescue Simulation League with my teammates. We participated in Khwarizmi Robotics Competition and got the second place in the country.

Then my friends and I concenterated on creating a Traffic Simulator, which simulated the traffic jam flow based on traffic lights' behaviour. This project ranked second place in Khwarizmi Young Award (Tehran) .

A year after that, I qualified to participate in Iranian National Olympiad in Informatics and I won the silver medal.

University

There is a competitive exam to enter the best universities in Iran which is called "Konkoor" . With my Khwarizmi award and medal in Informatics Olympiad, I applied for University of Tehran. I got admitted by passing an interview (without taking entrance exam - Konkoor) as talented student.

I continued studying AI and algorithms in university and I have passed Artificial Intelligence, Multi-Agent Systems, Social Networks Analysis and Game Theory courses.

I like problem solving specially in algorithms, so I participated as contestant in ACM/ICPC contest and ranked 9th in 2013 regional contest (SouthWest Asia). Moreover, I ranked 1st in the National University Students Olympiad in Iran.

I worked for a period in a company (Cafe Bazaar) as a developer. Bazaar is a Persian android marketplace and I was in a team that researched and developed the relation between apps and grouping them.

I am vice-chair of ACM student chapter in our university, and there are lots of activity being organized there: Scientific Talks, AI Contests, ICPC Contests and a lot more.
